The world map of sustainability

Global SDG status analysis with Data Visualization

Tableau worksheet available here

 The main goal of this project is to analyze and visualize the global sustainability development data. The dataset used for this project is Sustainable Development Goals sourced from The World Bank

Main topics I would like to explore on this project:
  1. GDP status and history by continent
  2. What are the top 10 countries producing/consuming green energy?
  3. What are the top 10 countries that have the largest CO2 emmisions
  4. How is the forest depletion trend for the past 20 years?

Project skill goals

  • Exploratory Data Analaysis
  • Data Visualization

Used tools

  • Tableau


CONCLUSION

  Climate change and sustainable development have been very important problems to solve for many countries these days. Today, 193 Parties (192 countries plus the European Union) have joined the Paris Agreement. The Agreement includes commitments from all countries to reduce their emissions and work together to adapt to the impacts of climate change and calls on countries to strengthen their commitments over time.

  While some countries are on the right track to achieve their goal of The Paris agreement, others still need to put more effort. By visualizing the data, some interesting factors are found as below:

GDP
  • Europe has the highest GDP per capita compared to others and always has been the top continent. Throughout history, GDP per capita was increasing for all continents.
  • Asia and North America seem to compete for taking 3rd rank of GDP per capita.
Renewable/Green energy
  • The green energy consumption chart shows most of the countries in Africa has a high green energy consumption ratio to total energy consumption. It is also possibly correlated to the fact that countries in Africa have less access to energy compared to the other continent.
  • The 'Lesotho' energy is 100% running on renewable energy followed by Paraguay, Bhutan, and Iceland.
CO2 Emission
  • China has the highest annual CO2 emissions amount followed by the United States, India, and Russian Federation.
  • Canada ranked 7th for CO2 emissions, however, among the 10 countries Canada produce renewable energy the most.
  • Among the top 10 CO2 emissions countries, South Korea produced the least renewable energy.
Forest depletion
  • The top 10 countries with highest forest depletion ratio were all african countries.
  • were all African countries. Burundi was the top country with the highest forest depletion rate.
  • Although most of the top 10 countries' forest depletion rates are either remaining at the same level or decreasing, Liberia's depletion rate has been increasing steadily.
  • In 2017, All countries' forest depletion rate has changed their directions to downhill.
